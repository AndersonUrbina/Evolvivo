An Introduction to Mindfulness Meditation

By Kenji Tanaka January 21, 2025 7 min read

In a world filled with constant distractions and demands on our attention, the practice of mindfulness offers a powerful antidote. Mindfulness is the basic human ability to be fully present, aware of where we are and what we’re doing, and not overly reactive or overwhelmed by what’s going on around us. Mindfulness meditation is the formal practice of training that ability.

The Core of the Practice: The Breath

The simplest way to begin meditating is by focusing on your breath. It's an anchor to the present moment that is always with you. Here’s a simple exercise to get you started:

  1. Find a comfortable seat. You can sit on a chair with your feet flat on the floor or on a cushion on the ground. Keep your back straight but not stiff.
  2. Gently close your eyes. Bring your attention to the physical sensation of your breath. Notice the feeling of the air entering your nostrils, filling your lungs, and then leaving your body.
  3. Your mind will wander. This is completely normal and expected. When you notice your mind has drifted to thoughts, plans, or worries, gently and without judgment, guide your attention back to your breath.
  4. Start small. Begin with just 5 minutes a day. Consistency is more important than duration.
"Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally." — Jon Kabat-Zinn

The Benefits of a Consistent Practice

The benefits of regular mindfulness practice are backed by extensive scientific research. It has been shown to reduce stress, improve focus, increase emotional regulation, and enhance overall well-being. By practicing mindfulness, you are not trying to stop your thoughts or empty your mind. Instead, you are learning to observe them without getting carried away. This simple shift in perspective can bring a profound sense of peace and clarity to your everyday life.
